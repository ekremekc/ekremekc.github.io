As we are interested in optimization problems in engineering, we need to represent the geometry of the system and simulate the relevant physics through various tools. For numerical solutions, we heavily use finite element method when discretizing the partial differential equations.

<br/><img src='/images/projects/fem/bogaz_mesh.png' width='600'>

We aim to be licence-independent when using the numerical tools and hence specialize in the applications of [FEniCS project](https://fenicsproject.org/) and its components. Some of them are; [Ubuntu/Linux](https://ubuntu.com/), [FEniCSx](https://github.com/FEniCS), [PETSc](https://petsc.org/release/overview/), [SLEPc](https://slepc.upv.es/), [Gmsh](https://gmsh.info/), [Python](https://www.python.org/) and [ParaView](https://www.paraview.org/).
